1-MIN → 15-MIN Aggregation Guide

How NautilusTrader's TimeBarAggregator turns 1-MINUTE-{BID,ASK}-EXTERNAL bars into 15-MINUTE-{BID,ASK}-INTERNAL bars.

Worked from real April 2024 EURUSD.FOREX_MS CSVs — every OHLCV number below is reproducible.

What this document shows. Three things, end-to-end:
  1. The bracket convention Nautilus uses to slot 1-min source bars into 15-min target windows: (left, right] — left-exclusive, right-inclusive.
  2. A sample from the actual raw_engine_data_1min_2024_04.csv source and the sniffer_strategy_15min_2024_04.csv aggregator output (full 12-column schema, OHLCV exposed).
  3. A fully worked window where the 15 source bars are listed alongside the single aggregated bar they produced — for both BID and ASK price types.

1. Source & target BarTypes

The aggregator's job is to take a stream of 1-minute bars that arrived from outside the engine, and emit a stream of 15-minute bars assembled inside the engine.
Technically: Two BarTypes per side are involved. The EXTERNAL tag means the bars were ingested as already-formed bars (from CSV, in our case). The INTERNAL tag means Nautilus's TimeBarAggregator built them at runtime by buffering the source bars. The composite form …-15-MINUTE-X-INTERNAL@1-MINUTE-EXTERNAL tells Nautilus exactly which feed to listen to.
Role BarType string Source Bars in April 2024
Source EURUSD.FOREX_MS-1-MINUTE-BID-EXTERNAL CSV → engine.add_data(...) 31,680
Source EURUSD.FOREX_MS-1-MINUTE-ASK-EXTERNAL CSV → engine.add_data(...) 31,680
Target EURUSD.FOREX_MS-15-MINUTE-BID-INTERNAL Built by TimeBarAggregator 2,880
Target EURUSD.FOREX_MS-15-MINUTE-ASK-INTERNAL Built by TimeBarAggregator 2,880

Counts: April has 30 days × 24 h × 4 fifteen-minute windows = 2,880 bars per side. April has 30 days × 24 h × 60 min = 43,200 minute-slots, but only 31,680 are populated — weekend gaps explain the difference.

2. The bracket: (left, right]

A 15-minute bar stamped with time T covers the source bars whose stamp falls after T − 15 min and up to and including T itself. The left endpoint is open (excluded); the right endpoint is closed (included).
Technically: Nautilus's TimeBarAggregator labels each emitted bar with its close timestamp. A 15-min bar at ts_event=T aggregates every 1-min bar with ts_event ∈ (T − 15 min, T]. In set notation: the half-open interval {x : T−15 min < x ≤ T}. The volume is summed; open is the first bar's open; close is the last bar's close; high/low are the extrema across the window.

Why the first bar is partial. The very first 15-min window would nominally start at T − 15 min = 23:45 on the previous day, but no source data exists before 00:00. So the bar at T=00:00 aggregates only one 1-min source bar (the one at 00:00 itself). That's not a bug — it's the (left, right] bracket faithfully consuming whatever bars happen to fall inside it. See section 7.

3. CSV schema (both files)

Both CSVs share an identical 12-column schema. The columns most relevant to aggregation are highlighted.

# Column Type Role
1 stream str raw_engine for the source CSV, sniffer_strategy for the aggregated CSV
2 price_type str BID or ASK
3 bar_type str Full Nautilus BarType string — see §1
4 ts_event_ns int64 Bar close timestamp, ns since epoch. This is the bracket's right edge.
5 ts_init_ns int64 When the bar object was constructed (= ts_event_ns in this dataset)
6 open float OHLC — Open of the window
7 high float OHLC — High of the window
8 low float OHLC — Low of the window
9 close float OHLC — Close of the window
10 volume float Summed volume across the window
11 ts_event str (ISO) Human-readable form of ts_event_ns
12 ts_init str (ISO) Human-readable form of ts_init_ns

7. Edge case — the very first bar (T = 00:00)

The aggregator's bracket for the first 15-min bar is (23:45 on March 31, 00:00 on April 1]. Nothing exists before 00:00 in the source CSV. So only one source row falls inside the window — the bar at 00:00 itself — and the 15-min bar is just a copy of that single 1-min bar.
Side source bar @ 00:00:00 15-min bar @ 00:00:00 Equal?
BID o=1.07932 h=1.07934 l=1.07918 c=1.07921 v=144 o=1.07932 h=1.07934 l=1.07918 c=1.07921 v=144 YES
ASK o=1.07934 h=1.07937 l=1.07921 c=1.07924 v=73 o=1.07934 h=1.07937 l=1.07921 c=1.07924 v=73 YES
Implication for downstream code. If your strategy treats the first 15-min bar as a "full" 15-min bar (e.g. computes an indicator that assumes 15 minutes of underlying volume), it will be looking at one minute of data wearing a 15-min label. The arithmetic is still correct by the (left, right] rule — it just happens to span a single minute. Either drop the first bar or warm up your indicator past the first window.

8. How to reproduce

Run the validation notebook end-to-end:

jupyter nbconvert --to notebook --execute \
    ipynb/individual_timeframes_validation_scripts/validate_aggregation_1min_to_15min.ipynb

It will write three CSVs to output/: the re-serialized source, the DataEngine-dispatched view, and the aggregated 15-min strategy view — and run four explicit assertions (engine.data correctness, sniffer ≡ source row-for-row, 15-min count band, Decimal-exact aggregation math on sampled windows). A passing run prints:

PASS A1: engine.data has 31,680 BID + 31,680 ASK 1-MIN-EXTERNAL bars
PASS A2: sniffer_engine row-for-row identical to source CSV (63,360 rows)
PASS A3: 15-MIN INTERNAL bars received — BID=2880  ASK=2880  (data-driven lower≈2112, clock-driven upper=2885)
PASS A4: aggregation math exact for 6 sampled 15-min windows (Decimal compare)
Bottom line. The 1-MINUTE-{BID,ASK}-EXTERNAL → 15-MINUTE-{BID,ASK}-INTERNAL pipeline is correct and Decimal-exact under the (left, right] bracket: 15 source bars in, 1 aggregate out (OHLCV preserved, volume summed) — with the single-source-bar first window being a faithful application of the same rule, not a special case.
